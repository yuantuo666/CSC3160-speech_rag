                  Consonants: Mannar of articulation
   ‚Ä¢ Consonants are also distinguished by how the restriction in

                                       Phone and Phoneme
  Definitions
  ‚Ä¢ Phoneme: an abstract, contrastive sound category in a language
          ‚Äîthe unit native speakers treat as ‚Äúthe same sound.‚Äù
       ‚Ä¢ Notation: /‚Ä¶/ (slashes), e.g., /t/, /i/.
       ‚Ä¢ Determined by minimal pairs (e.g., pin vs bin ‚Üí /p/ vs /b/ are different phonemes in English).

  ‚Ä¢ Phone: an actually produced speech sound, regardless of whether it ºs contrastive.
       ‚Ä¢ Notation: [‚Ä¶] (square brackets), e.g., [t ∞], [tÃ™], […æ].

  Relationship (allophones)
  ‚Ä¢ A phoneme can have multiple phones as its allophones
    ‚Äîcontext-dependent variants that don ºt change meaning in that language.
  ‚Ä¢ English /t/ ‚Üí [t ∞] (aspirated in top), [t] (unaspirated after /s/ in stop),
    […æ] (flap in water AmE), [tÃö] (unreleased in cat).
    These are phones (surface realizations) of the phoneme /t/.

  How they differ
  ‚Ä¢ Contrast vs. realization: phonemes contrast in meaning; phones realize phonemes.
  ‚Ä¢ Language-specific: the same phone can belong to different phonemes in different languages.
