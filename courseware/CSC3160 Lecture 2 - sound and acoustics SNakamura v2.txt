Loudness = volume?
‚Ä£ Loudness is the noise level perceived by an individual, whereas volume is an absolute
  sound level that can be scientifically measured. For example, if your family is
  watching a movie together, the TV volume is the same for everyone in the room.
  However, the TV's loudness may be much less for a person with a hearing
  impairment than it is for a person with normal hearing.


‚Ä£ If you increase the volume on a television, it will also incrementally increase the
  loudness of the sound. However, increasing the volume will not increase the loudness
  to the same degree for every person.
