                                              Source
  ‚Ä¢ Voicing source: Vocal folds vibrating
        ‚Ä¢ A periodic source produced by the modulation of the airflow
          from the lungs by the vocal folds
              ‚Ä¢ The vocal folds are muscular folds located in the larynx
        ‚Ä¢ If the vocal folds are close together, then air pressure from the
          lungs can cause them to vibrate periodically, generating
          voicing.
  ‚Ä¢ Unvoicing source: vocals fold holds close but not vibrating

                                          Frequency vs pitch
  ‚Ä¢ Frequency of the vocal folds refers to the actual physical phenomenon
  ‚Ä¢ Resonances in the vocal tract can emphasize harmonics of the fundamental frequency
    such that the harmonics are louder than the fundamental frequency
  ‚Ä¢ The perceived pitch is then the frequency of the harmonic instead of the fundamental
    frequency.

                  Independence of source and filter
  ‚Ä¢ Source
        ‚Ä¢ Fundamental frequency (F0) is driven by the frequency of vocal fold
          vibrations
        ‚Ä¢ Harmonics are multiples of F0
  ‚Ä¢ Filter
        ‚Ä¢ Resonances are driven by the shape of the vocal tract (physical property)
        ‚Ä¢ Formants are peaks in the spectral envelope that correspond to resonances
          (acoustic property)
  ‚Ä¢ Independence of source and filter
        ‚Ä¢ You can change F0 without changing the vowel you are saying: harmonics
          change, formants stay the same
