                               Windowing for analysis
‚Ä¢ A signal of a sound/speech/music is a sequence of acoustic events/words/lyrics
  etc.
‚Ä¢ The signal of a sound/speech/music is time-variant in character.
‚Ä¢ To extract information from a signal, we must therefore split the signal into
  sufficiently short segments
‚Ä¢ Extract segments which are short enough that the properties of the
  sound/speech/music signal does not have time change within that segment.
‚Ä¢ Windowing is a method in signal processing and it refers to splitting the input
  signal into temporal segments

                     Fundamental Assumptions
‚Ä¢ Properties of speech signal change relatively slowly with time (5-
  10 sounds per second)
  ‚Ä¢ Short 5-20 msec intervals
       ‚Ä¢ due to the small amount of data, varying pitch, varying amplitude
  ‚Ä¢ Medium 20-100 msec intervals
       ‚Ä¢ due to changes in sound quality, transitions between sounds, rapid transients in Speech

  ‚Ä¢ Long 100-500 msec intervals
       ‚Ä¢ Due to large amount of sound changes

‚Ä¢ There is always uncertainty in short-time measurements and
 estimates from speech signals
